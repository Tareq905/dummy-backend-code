SKRHYB App Design Sync
Tue, Aug 12, 2025

Summary:

The discussion centered on the design and functionality of the SKRHYB app, focusing on branding, user onboarding, and essential features. Wale Agbaje and Sahibzada Hassan Mansoor Bugvi reviewed initial design concepts, including the logo, color scheme, and typeface. They emphasized the importance of a streamlined onboarding process for both subscribers and businesses, highlighting identity verification and accurate business information. Wale proposed integrating Google API to facilitate the onboarding experience by pre-filling business details, which would enhance user engagement and simplify the process.

A significant aspect of the conversation involved the development of a simulation tool for service providers, allowing them to estimate potential earnings based on their service pricing and subscriber numbers. This feature aims to create an interactive experience similar to fitness apps, encouraging users to engage with the app from the beginning. Sahibzada supported this idea, and both participants discussed the importance of aesthetics and user experience in driving engagement.

Wale provided an overview of the business profile features, which include essential details such as business name, logo, services, and customer ratings. They discussed the need for a robust booking system that allows users to manage appointments effectively, as well as the importance of clear billing practices for missed appointments. Analytics and insights for businesses were also highlighted as crucial for tracking performance and facilitating loan applications. The conversation underscored the necessity of clear terms and conditions to prevent disputes between subscribers and service providers.

The team explored various subscription models, including monthly, quarterly, and annual options, along with a referral program to enhance user retention. Wale introduced the "Smart Budget Match" feature, which allows users to find services within their budget, and discussed personalization through service recommendations and flexible subscription plans. They also addressed the need for a dynamic calendar for service providers to manage their availability effectively. Overall, the discussion was productive, with both participants agreeing to continue refining the app's features and design in future sessions.
